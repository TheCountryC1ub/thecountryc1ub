Elite Golf Consulting × Foresome: Two Apps, One Mission — Love the Game

Growth takes time. A solid relationship can be instant. We're partnering with Barrett Edri and Foresome to keep golf fun, fair, and honest — track the bets, protect the handicaps, and never argue the math again.

By Cameron Tennant · July 7, 2026 · 5 min read

Some announcements you sit on for weeks trying to find the right words. This one is easy, because the whole thing can be said in a sentence: Elite Golf Consulting is partnering with Foresome — the golf platform built by Barrett Edri, its co-founder and CEO — because we're chasing the exact same thing from two different tees. They want you to love the game. We want you to master it. Turns out those are the same mission.

I've come to believe something about this sport and about business: growth takes time, but a solid relationship can be instant. You know it the moment you meet someone who sees golf the way you do. That's what happened here. No six-month courtship, no committee. Just two builders who looked at each other's work and said — yes, that's the game we're trying to protect.

Who is Foresome?

Foresome is a golf media platform and app built on a simple, slightly radical idea: golf is the greatest connection machine ever disguised as a sport, and it should belong to everyone who plays it — even the ones who can't break 100 yet. Their tagline says it better than I can: Love the game. Anyone. Anywhere.

Barrett Edri built it the way the best things in golf get built — out of love for the people you play with. He's a husband, father, author, and entrepreneur who treats connection like it's the whole point, because it is. When we started talking, it took about five minutes to realize we were describing the same golfer: someone who wants the game to be fun, competitive, and honest — all three, all the time.

The oldest argument in golf ends on the app

Every regular game has that moment. The round was great, the company was better, and then everyone's standing in the parking lot doing arithmetic. Who pressed on 14? Was the skin on 7 carried or halved? How many strokes does he get on the back? The bet that was supposed to make the round more fun becomes the thing everyone argues about after it.

Foresome kills that argument for good. Set up your round, pick your games — Nassau, Skins, Stableford — enter scores hole by hole, and the app handles everything else, handicaps built in. The bets track themselves. The points tally themselves. Nobody re-litigates the math over the trunk of a car. Their line for it is perfect: track the bet, not the math — so your foursome can love the game and never argue the arithmetic.

That matters more than it sounds. The money game is one of golf's oldest traditions — it's how four strangers become four friends over eighteen holes. Anything that removes friction from that tradition isn't a gimmick. It's stewardship.

Competition is only fun when the numbers are honest

Here's where our two missions lock together. A great money game runs on one thing: valid handicaps. The moment someone's number is soft — sandbagged down or vanity'd up — the whole thing curdles. The bets stop being fun because they stop being fair.

Foresome keeps the game honest on the scorecard: handicaps applied correctly, every stroke where it belongs, every payout clean. Elite Golf Consulting keeps you honest with yourself: tracking your rounds across the nine pillars of a great round, journaling what actually happened out there instead of what you wish had happened. When you genuinely know your game, your handicap stops being a negotiation and starts being a fact. Honest golfers make honest games. Honest games make golf worth playing for something.

Prestige and competition aren't opposites

There's a version of "growing the game" that flattens everything that made golf special in the first place. That's not what either of us is doing. The class of this sport — the etiquette, the handshake on the first tee, the way you putt everything out when money's on the line — that's not gatekeeping. That's the product.

What Barrett understands, and what we built Elite Golf Consulting around, is that you can hold the door open without lowering the standard. Welcome everyone. Keep the traditions. Play for something. The prestige of golf and the fun of golf were never in conflict — they were only ever separated by bad math and soft numbers, and we're fixing both.

The right people make the game easier to master

I'll leave you with the real reason this partnership exists. Nobody masters golf alone. I learned that at San Diego State, surrounded by players better than me — you improve faster around the right golfers, full stop. Their standards pull yours up. Their honesty makes yours easier. Their company makes the grind feel like a gift.

That's what both of these apps are really for. Foresome puts the right people around you and makes the competition fun and fair. Elite Golf Consulting makes sure every round with them moves you forward. Surround yourself with people who love the game, keep the numbers honest, play for something — and mastery stops being a mountain and starts being a walk with friends.

Growth takes time. This relationship was instant. Go see what Barrett and the Foresome team are building at foresome.com — and bring your foursome.
